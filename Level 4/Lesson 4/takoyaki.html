What is Takoyaki?

Takoyaki are crispy-outside, gooey-inside octopus balls from Osaka. These bite-sized snacks are made with wheat batter, filled with tender octopus pieces, and topped with savory sauce and dancing bonito flakes.

Origin & History

  • Born in Osaka, 1935 by Tomekichi Endo
  • Evolved from choboyaki and akashiyaki snacks
  • Spread nationwide after World War II
  • Now a festival and street food icon
